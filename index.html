The Science Behind The Relief

Why heat actually works for cramps

Period cramps happen when uterine muscles contract and cut off oxygen. Here's how Menstrela interrupts that cycle.

Heat relaxes muscles

Gentle heat increases blood flow to the lower abdomen, relaxing the uterine muscles that cause cramping — the same reason a hot water bottle helps, but faster and hands-free.

Vibration breaks the pain signal

Gentle vibration stimulates nerve endings that compete with pain signals — similar to why rubbing a bump helps. Combined with heat, it works significantly faster than heat alone.
